Why Do We Use 3DMARK?

3DMark has established itself as a highly reputable benchmarking tool renowned for its wide variety of tests, which effectively assess the performance of CPUs and GPUs and more. It offers a wide range of realistic gaming scenarios and stress tests ensuring that the results obtained are trustworthy and accurate. Once the benchmarks are complete, it validates the score ensuring autheticity. Additionally, 3DMark allows users to compare their system's performance against a huge database of user submitted results, enabling them to make well-informed decisions when it comes to selecting the most suitable CPUs and GPUs and more, articularly for gaming purposes.

User's can also visit the website and download the 3DMark app here.

Why Do We Use FPS Numbers from Tom's Hardware?

Having been in the industry since 1996, Tom's Hardware is a well-established and trusted source for hardware reviews, benchmarks, and technical analysis in the field of computer hardware. They provide comprehensive and in-depth coverage of CPUs, GPUs, and other components, including gaming performance testing. By sourcing FPS (Frames Per Second) numbers from Tom's Hardware, we ensure that the gaming performance data used in our analysis is reliable and based on rigorous testing methodologies. Tom's Hardware conducts extensive gaming tests on a wide range of hardware configurations, using popular and demanding games, to provide accurate and representative FPS benchmarks. These FPS numbers from Tom's Hardware enable us to evaluate the gaming performance of different PCs objectively and make informed recommendations to users. By relying on Tom's Hardware's expertise and reputation in the industry, we can ensure that our assessments are grounded in reliable and credible data. Please Note: that not all data may not be 100% accurate as FPS numbers can vary based on many factors such as CPU and GPU combination, drivers, in game settings and more.

For more information and detailed hardware reviews and information, you can visit the Tom's Hardware website here.
